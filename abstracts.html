Presentation Types

Oral Presentation

Present your research in a 10-minute talk followed by 5 minutes of Q&A in front of an audience of peers.

What to Expect:

  • • 10-minute presentation time
  • • 5-minute Q&A session
  • • Scheduled in parallel sessions
  • • PowerPoint slides recommended
  • • Competitive selection process

Poster Presentation

Display your research on a poster board and engage in one-on-one discussions with congress attendees.

What to Expect:

  • • Dedicated poster session times
  • • Face-to-face discussions
  • • Standard size: A0 portrait
  • • Awards for best posters
  • • Great networking opportunity

Submission Guidelines

1

Abstract Structure

Abstracts should be structured with clear sections: Background/Objectives, Methods, Results, and Conclusions. Maximum 250-300 words.

2

Language

All abstracts must be submitted in English. Please ensure proper grammar and spelling before submission.

3

Authors & Affiliations

Include all author names, affiliations, and a presenting author designation. Corresponding author contact information is required.

4

Topic Selection

Choose the most appropriate topic area for your abstract from the conference tracks listed on the Program page.

5

Presentation Preference

Indicate your preference for oral or poster presentation. The scientific committee will make final decisions based on quality and program balance.

6

Review Process

All abstracts undergo blind peer review by the scientific committee. Acceptance is based on scientific merit, originality, and relevance.
